Pleiades Change Log: 2011-11-01 - 2011-11-31

New Place Resources

  • Alamanni
    An ancient Germanic people, whose name in modern works is sometimes also spelled Alemanni or Allemanni.
    Creators: H. Bender
    Contributors: G. Moosbauer, R. Talbert, M. Puhane, Jeffrey Becker, Sean Gillies, Tom Elliott
  • Bituriges Cubi
    A Gallic tribe.
    Creators: H.S. Sivan, R.W. Mathisen
    Contributors: R. Talbert, Sean Gillies, Tom Elliott, Jeffrey Becker
  • El Kab
    An ancient Egyptian site with settlements dating back to prehistoric and Pharaonic times. The site also features rock-cut tombs from the 18th Dynasty and two temples primarily in use from the Early Dynastic period to the Ptolemaic period.
    Creators: Iris Fernandez
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker, Sean Gillies, Tom Elliott
  • Hadrian's Wall (Cumbrian Coast)
    Line of Roman fortifications along Cumbrian Coast running from western terminus of the stone wall commonly referred to as Hadrian's Wall at Maia (Bowness-on-Solway) in the north to Gabrosentum (Moresby) in the south.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker, Sean Gillies, Tom Elliott
  • Kom el-Dikka
    Part of the ancient city of Alexandria where the Roman theater and baths are located.
    Creators: Iris Fernandez
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker, Sean Gillies, Tom Elliott
  • Mandubii
    A conferation of Gallic tribes.
    Creators: G.D. Woolf
    Contributors: R. Talbert, Sean Gillies, Tom Elliott, Jeffrey Becker
  • Podere Cosciano
    Etrusco-Roman farm site
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors: Sean Gillies, Tom Elliott
  • Samnites
    An ancient, Oscan-speaking people who lived in the central southern part of the Apennine mountains in Italy.
    Creators: I.E.M. Edlund Berry, A.M. Small
    Contributors: R. Talbert, Sean Gillies, Tom Elliott, Jeffrey Becker
  • Stanegate
    An important Roman military road built in what is now northern England, linking Corbridge in the east and Carlisle in the west. A later western addition may have linked the fort at Kirkbride.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker, Sean Gillies
  • Temple of Zeus at Olympia
    The Temple of Zeus at Olympia was built in the second quarter of the fifth century B.C. Libon of Elis served as architect.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker, Tom Elliott
    Contributors: Brady Kiesling, Sean Gillies
  • Turoni
    An ancient people who lived in western France just south of Caesarodunum, modern Tours.
    Creators: H.S. Sivan, R.W. Mathisen
    Contributors: Brian Turner, R. Talbert, Sean Gillies, Jeffrey Becker, B. Turner, Tom Elliott
  • Unnamed road station
    An unnamed road station located west of Ankyra, near Bahtıllı in modern Turkey.
    Creators: T. Drew Bear
    Contributors: R. Talbert, Tom Elliott, Sean Gillies
  • Volcae Arecomici
    A Gallic people of the Languedoc. Their chief town was Nemausus.
    Creators: S. Loseby
    Contributors: R. Talbert, Sean Gillies, Tom Elliott, Jeffrey Becker

  • Valvisciolo
    An Iron Age settlement and necropolis located in the Monti Lepini near the abbey of Valvisciolo, characterized by polygonal masonry terrace walls.

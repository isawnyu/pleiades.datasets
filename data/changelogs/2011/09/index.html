New Place Resources

  • Abu Simbel
    The site of two temples constructed by Ramesses II, one dedicated to the triad of Amun, Ra-Horakhty, and Ptah, as well as the deified Ramesses, and another dedicated to Hathor and the pharaoh's wife Nefertari. The temple was relocated between 1964 and 1968 to prevent its submersion under Lake Nasser after the construction of the Aswan High Dam.
    Creators: Nate Nagy
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker, Sean Gillies, Jonathan Fu
  • Anastasian Wall
    Also known as the "Long Wall of Thrace," this fortification line (nearly 60km in length) spanned the Thracian peninsula west of Constantinople. It was built in the 5th century CE and refurbished and modified by the emperor Anastasius in the early 6th century.
    Creators: C. Foss
    Contributors: R. Talbert, Sean Gillies, Tom Elliott, Jeffrey Becker
  • Argentariae Dalmaticae et Pannonicae

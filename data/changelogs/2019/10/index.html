New Place Resources

  • Aphrodision at Akrai
    Temple dedicated to Aphrodite constructed in the 6th century BCE at the Greek colony of Akrai.
    Creators: Gabriel McKee
    Contributors:
  • Archaic agora of Thasos
    The agora of the ancient city. The site was located near the center of the city's shoreline and was surrounded by stoas and public buildings.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Ashtead Roman villa
    The site of a Romano-British villa complex that included a bathhouse and tile works, occupied from ca. 67-79 CE until its abandonment ca. 200 CE.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Bardown Roman industrial site
    Site of Roman bloomery and iron mining pits, dating from the late 1st century CE to ca. 220.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Barnwood Roman cemetery
    Site of Romano-British inhumation and cremation cemetery.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Brislington Roman villa
    Site of a late third or fourth century winged corridor-type villa first excavated in 1899.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Broxtowe Roman fort
    Site of Roman fortification dating from 50-75 CE.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Caerphilly Roman fort
    Site of late 1st century CE Roman auxiliary fort between Cardiff and Gelligaer.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Combley Roman villa
    Site of Romano-British villa of winged corridor type, probably late 3rd century CE in date.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker

    Site of a bi-vallate Iron Age hill fort in present-day Herefordshire.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker

    Romano-British villa situated in present-day Gloucestershire
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • 'Ein-Shadud
    Small settlement in the Jezreel Valley containing structures dated to the Early Bronze Age I. Salvage excavations were conducted on the site in 1979 prior to its destruction by construction.
    Creators: Gabriel McKee
    Contributors:
  • Elginhaugh Roman fort
    Site of the most completely excavated timber-built auxiliary fort (with annexe) in the Roman Empire, situated in present-day Midlothian, Scotland. Roman occupation was short-lived, having been established in the early 70s CE and abandoned in or shortly after 86. Now completely built over by the Royal Bank of Scotland Data Centre.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Ermin Street
    A branch Roman road (Margary 41) running from Calleva Atrebatum (modern Silchester) to the legionary fortress at Col. Glevum (Gloucester). Not to be confused with the major north-south road named Ermine Street (Margary 2).
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Franks Roman villa
    The site of a Roman villa with two parallel ranges of rooms dating from the second half of the first century to the fifth century.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Green Ore
    Site of a Roman lead-working settlement at Rookery Farm, and findspot of a group of four lead ingots excavated in the 1950s.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Hacheston
    Site of a Romano-British settlement dating from the first-fourth centuries CE, located near modern Wickham Market in Suffolk.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Horncastle
    Site of lesser Roman walled town in present-day Lincolnshire, with occupation possibly as early as 1st century CE. The city walls, however likely do not predate the 4th century CE. Candidate location for Bannovallum (the other being Caistor).
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • House of the Centenary
    A Pompeian domus re-discovered in 1879, the so-called House of the Centenary is among the largest domus in Pompeii.
    Creators: Zoe Blecher-Cohen
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Lickfold
    Site of Roman bathhouse accompanying possible villa, mansio, or larger settlement, at Lickfold near Wiggonholt in West Sussex
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Listercombe Bottom
    The site of a Roman villa discovered ca. 1760 and excavated in 1930. It lies ca. 2.5 km SE of Chedworth Roman villa.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Little Farningham Roman iron production site
    A Romano-British iron working site located in the East Sussex Weald.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Lodge Farm Roman villa
    Site of a Romano-British villa with a substantial bathhouse near North Warnborough (Hampshire), likely occupied from mid-3rd to late-4th centuries CE.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Manor House Roman villa
    The site of a Roman corridor villa with a separate bathhouse. Three phases dating from the late first century AD into the fourth century. It possibly forms part of a complex with the villa uncovered ca. 250 meters to the south and east.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Mura Serviane, Anagni
    An ancient opus quadratum wall at Anagni referred to as "Mura Serviane" based on its construction technique.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Mycenaean cemetery at Aidonia
    Mycenaean burial ground located approximately 8 kilometers west of Nemea containing 20 tombs dated to the 15th century BCE. All but two of the burials were looted in the 1970s, but the illicit finds were retrieved and repatriated to Greece in 1996.
    Creators: Gabriel McKee
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker, Tom Elliott
  • Old Winteringham
    Site of extensive Roman occupation and possible Claudio-Neronian military base where Ermine Street crosses the river Humber, opposite the fort at Petuaria (Brough-on-Humber).
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Penydarren Roman fort
    Roman fort built ca. 74-78 CE and occupied no later than the first third of the second century, now built over by a sports stadium.
    Creators: Scott Vanderbilt
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Priddy

    Contributors:
  • Roman tilery at St Oswald's Priory
    Site of a second century CE Roman tile manufactory on the grounds of the later medieval priory of St. Oswald at Gloucester.

    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Royal Hypogeum at Qatna
    Tomb VI at Qatna, known as the Royal Hypogeum, is a tomb complex located approximately 12 meters below the Royal Palace of Qatna. The tomb consists of a long corridor and 4 burial chambers, and contained approximately 20 burials.
    Creators: Gabriel McKee
    Contributors:
  • Royal Palace of Qatna
    A large palace constructed in about 1600 BCE in Qatna, an important Bronze Age city in Syria.
    Creators: Gabriel McKee
    Contributors:
  • St Peters Church, Flawford
    A ruined Medieval church located on the site of a Roman villa.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Tell el-Hesi
    Archaeological site located in southern Palestine with signs of habitation (with gaps) from the late Neolithic through the Hellenistic period, as well as burials from Late Antiquity.
    Creators: Gabriel McKee
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Tell Qasile
    Archaeological site on the Yarkon River in modern Tel Aviv, identified as a Philistine city inhabited during the 12th and 11th centuries BCE.

  • Tomb of Quintus Sulpicius Maximus

  • Albanus M.
    The second highest summit of the Alban Hills, Mons Albanus is an extinct volcano with an elevation of 950 m (3,117 ft). In the middle of the first millennium B.C. there may have been volcanic activity as noted by the historian Livy. The mountain was sacred for Latin peoples who held important festivals there and a major shrine to Iuppiter Latiaris stands on the mountain top.

New Place Resources

  • Achaean League (Second)
    Established in 280 BCE, the second Achaean League was centered in the northern and central Peloponnese. Allied to Rome in the Second Macedonian War (200-196 BCE), the league extended its control over all of the Peloponnese. In the Third Macedonian War (171–168 BCE) the League's relationship with Rome deteriorated; in ca. 146 BCE this led to open warfare, which resulted in the defeat and dissolution of the league.
    Creators: Ryan M. Horne
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Aetolian League
    Based in Aetolia in central Greece, the Aetolian League was founded in the Hellenistic period to counter the power of Macedon and the Achaean League. It was allied with Rome in the First Macedonian War (215-205 BCE), but later was opposed to the republic in Rome's war against Antiochos (192–188 BCE). After Rome's victory in 188, the Aetolian League was fully subordinate to Rome.
    Creators: Ryan M. Horne
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker, Tom Elliott
  • Afyonkarahisar Castle
    Afyonkarahisar Castle is a Late Bronze Age fort (ca. 1350 B.C.) and may be the site of the Hittite settlement known as Hapanuwa.

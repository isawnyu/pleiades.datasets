New Place Resources

  • Aedes Fauni
    A temple dedicated to Faunus located on Tiber Island, the Aedes Fauni was dedicated in 196 BCE by Gnaeus Domitius Ahenobarbus and Gaius Scribonius Libo.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Amphitheater at Castrum Rauracense
    Discovered in late 2021 on the site of the Basler Ruderclub in Augst, this amphitheater lies just west of the Roman fort of Castrum Rauracense. It seems to have been built in an abandoned quarry sometime in the 4th century CE.
    Creators: Tom Elliott
    Contributors: Sebastian Heath
  • Aquae Rusellanae
    A thermal site located near Roman Rusellae in regular use and reuse since at least Roman times. Its ancient name is not known. The first modern excavations and mapping efforts date to the 18th century of the modern era.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors: Tom Elliott
  • Balineum Ampelidis et Dianes
    A bath complex that is included in Regio XIV and also possibly represented on the Severan Marble plan. In the Notitia, referred to as "Balineum Ampelidis, Prisci et Dianes" and as Balineum Ampelidis et Dianes in the Curiosum.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Basilica Ulpia
    The Basilica Ulpia is located in the Forum of Trajan at Rome and derives its name from the emperor Trajan's gentilician nomenclature. The basilica was considered a marvel of ancient Roman architecture by observers, including the historian Ammianus Marcellinus.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors: Brady Kiesling
  • Cannatello
    An archaeological site located in the territory of the eponymous frazione of the Italian municipality of Agrigento, Sicily. A middle and late Bronze Age fortified settlement dating to the fourteenth to thirteenth / twelfth centuries BCE has been discovered. Archaeologists deem it to be an important center of maritime trade. The site is located some seven kilometers southeast of the historic center of Agrigento and it sits on a flat hill located near the Naro River, about 1.5 kilometers from the coast.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors: Tom Elliott
  • Casa del Obispo
    The archaeological site of Casa del Obispo has evidence for multiple urban phases, ranging from the eighth century B.C. to the eighteenth century.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Circus Varianus
    A circus that was part of the Sessorium palace next to the Amphitheatrum Castrense. The obelisk currently situated on the Pincian Hill was discovered on the site in the sixteenth century. It had been moved to the spina of the circus during the reign of Elagabalus.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Column of Trajan
    The Column of Trajan (or Trajan's Column) is a victory column that was erected in the Forum of Trajan at Rome ca. 113 in order to commemorate Trajan's victories in the Dacian Wars. The column is particularly noted for its helical historiated bas relief frieze decoration.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors: Brady Kiesling
  • Delian League
    A modern name for an alliance formed by Greek cities, led by Athens, in 478 BCE. Formed in the aftermath of the Persian Wars, the purpose of the league was ostensibly to avenge damage to Greece inflicted by the Persian Empire by attacking Persian territory. Athens increasingly dominated the league until it effectively became the Athenian Empire.
    Creators: Ryan Horne
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Henchir Hnich
    Discovered in 2013, this site in the modern Oued Ellouz sector of Tunisia lies 3.5km west of ancient Musti (modern Henchir Mest). Excavators interpret the site as a rural settlement of an imperial estate. The only known copy of an inscribed "lex de agris rudibus" of the emperor Hadrian was discovered here (unpublished as of January 2022).
    Creators: Tom Elliott
    Contributors:
  • Horti Maiani
    An urban garden of imperial Rome located on the summit of the Esquiline Hill. Based on an inscription dating to the reign of Claudius, it is believed that these gardens were administered by the same magistrate in charge of the Horti Lamiani, an official with the title "procurator hortorum Maianorum et Lamianorum".
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors: Tom Elliott
  • Kangurttut
    Bactrian site in southwestern Tajikistan containing a settlement and burial ground dated to the Bronze and early Iron Ages.
    Creators: Gabriel Mckee
    Contributors:
  • Kopia
    Settlement and glassmaking site in northern India, inhabited and in use during the first millennium BCE.
    Creators: Gabriel Mckee
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Künzing Roman amphitheater
    A Roman timber amphitheater dating from the first to fifth centuries, the Künzing Roman amphitheater measured 46 by 40 meters. The site was discovered during excavations carried out in 2003 and 2004. The archaeological site is no longer visible but a reconstructed evocation of the ancient structure marks the location.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker; Tom Elliott
    Contributors:

  • Poggio Civitella
    The hill of Poggio Civitella near Montalcino, Italy, rises to a height of 661 meters above sea level. Beginning in 1993, excavation campaigns revealed a series of settlement horizons dating to the first millennium BCE, ranging from the archaic to the Hellenistic periods. The hilltop was fortified during this period.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Ponte sul Fosso Tre Ponti
    A Roman bridge on the Via Amerina between ancient Nepet and Falerii.
    Creators: W.V. Harris
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker; Sean Gillies; Tom Elliott; R. Talbert
  • Porticus Aemilia
    A utilitarian porticus and storage building located outside the Porta Trigemina, the Porticus Aemilia was erected in 193 BCE by Marcus Aemilius Lepidus and and Lucius Aemilius Paullus.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Porticus margaritaria
    A place located in regio VIII that was connected with jewelers. While the porticus itself is mentioned only in the Regionary Catalogues, numerous extant inscriptions attest to the shops of jewelers in the vicinity of the Sacra Via.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Regio XIII (Aventinus)
    The thirteenth administrative region of ancient Rome was created by Augustus in 7 BCE. This district contained 17 shrines (aediculae), 130 townhouses (domūs), 35 warehouses (horrea), 60 bath houses, and 88 fountains in addition to a list of monuments enumerated in the Regionary Catalogues.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Regio XIV (Transtiberim)
    The fourteenth regio of imperial Rome was created by the Augustan administrative reform of 7 BCE. This region contained, in addition to the named monuments, the statio of the seventh cohort of the Vigiles, 78 shrines (aediculae), 150 townhouses, 22 warehouses (horrea), 86 bathhouses, and 180 fountains. At the beginning of the fifth century, the Regio was subdivided into 78 districts or vici and 4,405 insulae.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Saint-Saturnin-du-Bois Gallo-Roman villa
    Remains of an extensive Gallo-Roman villa located in the modern French commune of Saint-Saturnin-du-Bois (Charente-Maritime department).
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors: Tom Elliott
  • Sanctuary of Poseidon at Tainaron
    A famous ancient sanctuary of Poseidon located at the southern end of the modern Mani peninsula of the Peloponnese. Literary and archaeological evidence indicate that the sanctuary featured a temple, a cave treated as a mouth of the underworld, and other structural and monumental components. It seems to have served as a sanctuary site for suppliants and a context for the formal manumission of enslaved persons.
    Creators: Tom Elliott
    Contributors:
  • Santa Maria in Tempulo
    A deconsecrated Christian church on the flank of Rome's Caelian Hill, Santa Maria in Tempulo is connected with the ancient tradition of the spring and grove of Camenae.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Su Coddu
    A prehistoric settlement covering some 10 hectares with evidence for approximately 150 huts located in Selargius, Sardinia.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors: Tom Elliott
  • Temple of Veiovis
    The Temple of Veiovis, also attested as Aedes Vediovis, was a temple of ancient Rome located on the Capitoline Hill in a location known as "inter duos lucos" that is interpreted to mean a saddle between the two summits of the hill. The remains of a temple podium were discovered beneath the northwest corner of the Palazzo Senatore in 1938-1939.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Tumulus de Péré
    The Péré tumulus, or Pairé tumulus, is a Neolithic tumulus that dates from the fifth millennium B.C. The site is located in Prissé-la-Charrière, in Deux-Sèvres, in New Aquitaine, France. It is composed of three main megalithic structures, built at different times.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors: Tom Elliott
  • Tytkesken-VI
    Multi-period burial site in the northern Altai region. The bulk of the burials on the site are dated to the Pazyryk period (6th-4th centuries BCE), with additional burials from the Byikenskaya (8th-6th centuries BCE), Bulan-Kobinskaya (2nd-3rd centuries CE), and Turkic (6th-8th centuries CE) cultures.
    Creators: Gabriel Mckee
    Contributors:
  • Via Ostiensis necropolis
    A necropolis at Ostia that began during the second century B.C. on the south side of the Via Ostiensis.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors: Tom Elliott

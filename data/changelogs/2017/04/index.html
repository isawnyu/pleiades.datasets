Pleiades Change Log: 2017-04-01 - 2017-04-31

New Place Resources

  • Bash Tapa
    Tell site located south of Erbil in northern Iraq inhabited from the Early Dynastic through the Middle Assyrian periods.
    Creators: Gabriel McKee
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Bestansur
    The archaeological site at Bestansur is in the Shahrizor Plain in the foothills of the Central Zagros mountains in modern Iraq. The tell, which lies just east and south of the modern village, shows evidence for extensive Early Neolithic occupation as well as later Neo-Assyrian and Sasanian activity.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors: Tom Elliott
  • Bilske Horodyshche
    Archaeological site near the village of Bilsk in Ukraine; possibly the site of Gelonos, identified by Herodotus as the capital city of the Budini.
    Creators: Gabriel McKee
    Contributors: Tom Elliott
  • Bratolyubovskii Kurgan
    A Scythian burial mound dated to the fourth century B.C. located near the village of Bratolyubivka in Kherson Oblast, Ukraine.
    Creators: Gabriel McKee
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Çadır Höyük
    An archaeological site in Yozgat province, Turkey, tentatively identified as the Hittite town Zippalanda (Uşaklı Höyük is another candidate for the location of Zippalanda).
    Creators: Gabriel McKee
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker, Tom Elliott
  • Cima tumulus
    A rock-cut Etruscan tomb dating to the second half of the seventh century B.C.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Coccium?
    A minor Romano-British settlement located at Wigan, Lancashire.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors: Tom Elliott, Scott Vanderbilt
  • Horti Sallustiani
    The gardens of Sallust in Regio VI of the ancient city of Rome
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors: Brady Kiesling
  • Kınık Höyük
    An archaeological site in southern Cappadocia. Inhabited from the Late Bronze Age through the Middle Iron Age, Kınık Höyük was an important settlement in the Kingdom of Tuwana.
    Creators: Gabriel McKee
    Contributors: Noah Kaye, Tom Elliott
  • Roman amphitheater at Porolissum
    An amphitheater that originated under Hadrian and that was then rebuilt in stone ca. A.D. 157.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Scarborough Castle
    The ruins of a medieval castle built ca. A.D. 1135 by William le Gros, Earl of Albemarle. Now an English Heritage site.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker, Tom Elliott
    Contributors: Charlotte Tupman
  • Tell el-Farkha
    Settlement near the village of Ghazalah in the eastern Nile Delta inhabited from the Naqada II through the Old Kingdom period.
    Creators: Gabriel McKee
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Tell Mohammad Diyab
    An archaeological site in northeastern Syria inhabited in the Early Dynastic through post-Akkadian period (ca. 2750-1800 BCE).
    Creators: Gabriel McKee
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Tumulo del tesoro
    An Etruscan tumulus of the seventh century B.C.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Uşaklı Höyük
    A Bronze and Iron Age site in Yozgat province, Turkey, tentatively identified as the Hittite town of Zippalanda (Çadır Höyük is another candidate for the location of Zippalanda).
    Creators: Gabriel McKee
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker, Tom Elliott
  • Villa Fuente Álamo
    A Roman villa at Puente Genil in the province of Córdoba in Spain.
    Creators: Tom Elliott
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Washing Wells Roman Fort
    The site of a Roman fort has been identified via aerial photos. It is situated in agricultural land on Dunston Hill overlooking the River Tyne, 3 miles southwest of Newcastle and ca. 1 km southeast of Whickham.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors: Gabriel Moss
  • Water Eaton Roman camps
    A series of Roman camps on the River Penk along the line of Watling Street.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Yassı Ada (island)
    An island bordering the Chuka Channel in the southeastern Aegean sea; two shipwreck sites (dating to the fourth and seventh centuries A.D.) are located off its southern coast.

    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker

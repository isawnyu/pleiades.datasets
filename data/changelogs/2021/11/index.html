  • Theopetra Cave
    Located in Thessaly, the Theopetra Cave is an important archaeological site with evidence of multi-phase prehistoric human occupation. Radiocarbon dates indicate human activity beginning at least 50,000 years ago. Archaeogenetics have focused on evidence related to Mesolithic inhabitants of the cave site.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors: Brady Kiesling
  • Thermae Helenianae
    The Thermae Helenianae or Thermae Helenae was a thermal complex located in the Horti Spei Veteris, also referred to as the Horti Variani. The baths were situated near the arcus Neroniani adjacent to the Amphitheatrum Castrense. As Helena, mother of Constantine I, restored them following a fire in 323-326, they are named for her.

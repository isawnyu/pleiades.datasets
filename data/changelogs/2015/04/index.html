New Place Resources

  • Agora at Malia
    The so-called "agora" is a large open courtyard to the northwest of the Middle Minoan palace at Malia.

    Creators: Kayne Khoury
    Contributors: Alexa Frazey, Jeffrey Becker, Julia Doncaster, Adam Rabinowitz, Carlos Martinez
  • Athenian Treasury at Delphi
    The Athenian Treasury at Delphi, built between 510 and 480 BC, is a building intended to house offerings made to Apollo by the citizens of Athens.
    Creators: Hannah Spock, Morgan Lubenow, Allison Schaub, JohnPaul Aguilar, John Tran
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker, Brady Kiesling, John Paul Aguilar, Adam Rabinowitz
  • Casal Bertone necropolis
    Casal Bertone is named for the modern Roman neighborhood where it was discovered, about 2 km east of the Porta Maggiore. The site of Casal Bertone covers more than 3,000 square meters. The eastern portion of the site comprised a residential area that was occupied from about the first century B.C. to the fifth to sixth centuries A.D.
    Creators: Jonathan Weiland, Sarah Bond
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Castellaccio Europarco
    A villa and a number of burials located along the Via Laurentina, dating from the fourth century BC to the second century AD.
    Creators: Jonathan Weiland
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Eremo di San Michele Arcangelo
    A rock-cut Christian sanctuary located on Monte Altino in the Monti Aurunci that was begun ca. A.D. 830.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:

  • Kato Phournos Tomb
    The Kato Phournos Tomb is one of the nine tholos, or "beehive", tombs found around the acropolis of Mycenae. It is thought to have been constructed between 1460 and 1400 BC.
    Creators: Alexa Frazey, Julia Doncaster
    Contributors: Kayne Khoury, Jeffrey Becker, Tom Elliott, Adam Rabinowitz, Carlos Martinez
  • Library of Pergamum

  • Temple of Aphrodite Euploia
    The temple of Aphrodite Euploia is a tholos constructed in the fourth century BC in Knidos, a coastal city on the Datça Peninsula. It was built to house Praxiteles' famous nude statue of Aphrodite.

    Contributors: Bianka Torres, Terry Orr, Adam Rabinowitz, Jeffrey Becker
  • The House of the Masks
    The House of the Masks (also known as the House of the Comedians), built around 150-100 BC, was a luxurious residential complex that may have housed traveling troops of actors performing at the theater in Delos.

    Contributors: Elise Johnson, Michael Horst, Jeffrey Becker, Emily van Zanten, Dalton Parish, Adam Rabinowitz, Tom Elliott
  • The Theater of Larissa
    The theater at Larissa was a large stone theater built around the end of the third century BC and used for dramatic performances and as a political meeting place.
    Creators: Michael Horst
    Contributors: Elise Johnson, Michael Horst, Jeffrey Becker, Emily van Zanten, Dalton Parish, Adam Rabinowitz
  • Throne Room in Palace of Minos
    The Throne Room in the "Palace of Minos" at Knossos, a room with ritual features located on the west side of the central courtyard of the palace. The room takes its name from the masonry throne, flanked by frescoes depicting griffins, that occupies one of its walls.
    Creators: McKenna Hubbard, Hannah Wilmeth (hwilmeth)
    Contributors: Terry Orr, Bianka Torres, Adam Rabinowitz, Tom Elliott
  • Unit IV-1 at Nichoria
    Unit IV-1 is a large, megaron-style building constructed during the 11th century BCE at the site of Nichoria in Messenia, Greece.
    Creators: McKenna Hubbard, Hannah Wilmeth (hwilmeth)
    Contributors: Terry Orr, Bianka Torres, Adam Rabinowitz, Jeffrey Becker
  • Valcamonica
    Located in the Lombardy plain, the Valcamonica assemblage constitutes one of the world's greatest collections of prehistoric petroglyphs – created over a period of 8,000 years. The site was enlisted by UNESCO in 1971.
    Creators: Jeffrey Becker
    Contributors:
  • Vallerano
    A necropolis located on the southern outskirts of Rome. The necropolis is located near a villa site along a road running through the suburbium of Rome. Excavated between 1992 and 1993.
    Creators: Jonathan Weiland
    Contributors: Jeffrey Becker
  • Vapheio Tholos Tomb
    The Vapheio Tholos Tomb is a Mycenaean tholos built around 1500 BC that is assumed to have held an elite burial or burials. It is best known for the two golden Minoan-style cups it housed.

    Contributors: Elise Johnson, Brady Kiesling, Sean Gillies, Michael Horst, Jeffrey Becker, Emily van Zanten, Adam Rabinowitz, Tom Elliott, Dalton Parish
